The Book That Never Ends

What Jesus Began  ·  Acts 28:30–31

Hook

Every good story has an ending. So what does it mean when the most important one doesn't?

Devotional

The book of Acts covers thirty years of Christ followers being shipwrecked, arrested, run out of cities, and somehow changing the world anyway.

And then it just… stops.

"He lived there two whole years at his own expense, and welcomed all who came to him, proclaiming the kingdom of God and teaching about the Lord Jesus Christ with all boldness and without hindrance."

— Acts 28:30-31

No conclusion. No epilogue. Luke — the most careful writer in the New Testament — just sets his pen down mid-story.

The simplest answer might be the most unsettling one: the book doesn't end because the story doesn't end. It was meant to be an open door. The same Spirit that filled a room in Jerusalem and gave courage to ordinary people — that story kept going. Through centuries. Through Nashville.

Through you, if you let it.

Application

Most of us treat faith — or even the search for meaning — like a book we either finished or put down.

What if the reason nothing has felt quite finished is because you're standing in the middle of a story that's still being written?
