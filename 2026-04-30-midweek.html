You Can't Fill Yourself

What Jesus Began  ·  Acts 2:1–4

Hook

There's a version of tired that sleep doesn't fix. Have you ever felt completely empty and had no idea why?

Devotional

There's a moment in Acts 2 that's easy to miss if you're moving too fast.

Picture this. There's a room full of ordinary people. Fishermen. Mothers. Nobody impressive. They've been waiting ten days on a promise Jesus made before He ascended... and that something was coming.

"And suddenly there came from heaven a sound like a mighty rushing wind, and it filled the entire house where they were sitting."

— Acts 2:2

Wind. Fire. And people who walked in empty walked out speaking languages they'd never learned.

But here's the part that gets me. The wind of Genesis breathed life into dust. The wind of Acts breathed power into people who were already alive. There's a difference between existing and being fueled.

Most of us are existing. We've been existing for a while. And we've been trying to fix that by working harder.

Application

You've tried the new routine. The early mornings. The self-help. The hustle.

And some of it works — for a while. Until the week hits different and the tank is just empty again.

Here's what Acts is saying to that: a tank cannot fill itself. That was never how this was supposed to work. The Spirit that showed up in that room in Jerusalem wasn't a reward for the people who had it together. It filled the ones who had run out of options.

That might be the best news you hear this week.
