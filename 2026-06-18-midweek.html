Lead Into Gold

Philippians  ·  Philippians 1:12

Hook

What if the thing you're most ashamed of isn't something to hide, but the exact raw material God uses to make something good?

Devotional

Paul wrote the letter to the Philippians from the bottom of his life — chained to a Roman soldier, his ministry frozen, an execution likely waiting at the end of it. And yet the first thing he says about that season is almost impossible to believe.

"What has happened to me has really served to advance the gospel."

— Philippians 1:12

He's calling the worst stretch of his life forward motion, and it actually was. The guard shackled to his wrist couldn't walk away, which meant the most persuasive man alive suddenly had a captive audience for hours at a time. Rome thought it was holding a prisoner, but it was really running an evangelism shift.

For a thousand years, brilliant people chased the same impossible dream — turning lead into gold. Kings funded it, even Isaac Newton lost years to it, and no one ever pulled it off. But God seems to do it before breakfast. He took a prison cell and made it a pulpit, took an iron chain and made it a sermon. That's not a trick or a one-time miracle. It's just who he is.

Application

Everybody has a season they'd erase if they could — the breakup, the bad quarter, the year that didn't make any highlight reel. The instinct is always to bury it and get out in front of the story before anyone notices.

But sit with one honest question this week: what if that thing isn't wasted at all, but the very lead the alchemist is reaching for? You can't turn it to gold yourself — that's the whole point. The hands that do it were never meant to be yours.
