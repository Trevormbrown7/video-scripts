_Presence You Can't Download_

What Jesus Began  ·  Acts 1:1–11

Hook

Have you ever felt alone in moments when you're the most connected?

(-Walk off screen-)

Notifications on. Feeds running. Technically reachable by anyone, anywhere — and somehow the silence inside gets louder.

That's not a you problem. That's a wiring problem. You weren't made for digital presence. You were made for something else entirely.

Devotional

There's a moment in Acts 1 where Jesus ascends into a cloud, and eleven of his closest friends just stand there. Staring at an empty sky.

"You will receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you, and you will be my witnesses."

— Acts 1:8

He's not leaving them alone. He's changing the mode.

Charles Spurgeon pointed out something that's hard to shake once you see it: while Jesus walked the roads of Galilee, he could only be in one place. One conversation. One village. One century.

By ascending — he goes from being somewhere to being everywhere. His absence isn't abandonment. It's amplification.

Application

You don't have to be religious to understand this.

Most of us are running some version of the same experiment — more connection, more content, more noise — hoping that somewhere in the feed, we'll feel less alone. And it continues to disappoint.

What the Ascension announces is that the presence you're actually looking for isn't coming from a screen. It's not another follow, another notification, another person finally seeing you.

It's older than any of that. And it's closer than you think.
