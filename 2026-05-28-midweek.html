Silence Does The Damage

What Jesus Began  ·  Acts 6:1–7

Hook

Nashville is full of people keeping score in silence. What if the silence is doing more damage than the problem ever could?

Devotional

The earliest church in history almost split apart. Not over theology. Not over persecution. But, over logistics.

Widows were being overlooked in the daily food distribution. It was a real problem, but the more dangerous thing was what Luke calls "a secret displeasure not openly declared." Nobody brought it up. They just whispered about it.

"A complaint by the Hellenists arose against the Hebrews because their widows were being neglected in the daily distribution."

— Acts 6:1

Paul Levy put it this way: grumbling is a sign that your eyes are on Egypt and not on the Promised Land. It's not that the frustration isn't valid. It's that carrying it in silence turns it into something corrosive.

What saved them wasn't pretending everything was fine. The apostles called everyone together, named the problem, and solved it — no ego, no defensiveness.

Application

You know what this feels like. Something's off at work, in a friendship, at home and instead of saying it, you keep a quiet tally. You're not angry. You're "fine." But the silence compounds.

Most things don't blow up because the problem was too big. They blow up because nobody said anything while it was still small.
