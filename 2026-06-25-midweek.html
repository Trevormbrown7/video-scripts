Twenty Years, Already Singing

Philippians  ·  Philippians 2:11

Hook

The oldest song the church owns is a worship song to a man Rome executed. And these were the lyrics.

Devotional

When Paul wrote to Philippi around AD 62, he slipped in a few lines that don't sound like the rest of the letter. Paul is quoting something the churches already sang by heart. Which means the words were older than the letter, sung by ordinary people within a generation of the execution.

Here's the line they landed on:

"…and every tongue confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father."

— Philippians 2:11

The word "Lord"... Paul lifts that word from the holiest sentence in Israel's scriptures and sets it on a carpenter Rome had nailed to a cross. There was nothing more outrageous you could say — and the people singing it were the least likely on earth to ever call a man God.

Application

It would be easy to assume a claim like this grew over time — that someone made it up long after the fact. But it shows up early, from the very people who had the most to lose by saying it. People don't risk everything for a story they know they invented. They risk it for something they're convinced is true.
