_The Direction of the Arrow_

What Jesus Began  ·  Acts 2:42–47

Hook

Most of us are wired to receive before we give. It's not a character flaw, it's just the default.

But what if the default is exactly what's making us miserable?

Devotional

In Acts 2, something strange is happening in Jerusalem. Three thousand people are suddenly selling things. Giving things away. Eating in each other's homes. Nobody told them to. Nobody passed a law.

"All the believers were together and had everything in common. They sold property and possessions to give to anyone who had need."

— Acts 2:44-45

Charles Spurgeon had a line for this. He said: a communist says, all yours is mine. A Christian says, all mine is yours. The difference isn't the amount. It's the direction of the arrow.

The early church gave because they had received something so large they couldn't keep their hands closed anymore. They had been given, completely and at great cost, by someone who had every reason not to. And that changed the direction they faced.

Application

You don't have to believe anything to feel the pull of this.

There have been studies done, confirming what most of us quietly know: the more we give, the better we feel. The more we protect what we have, the more anxious we get about losing it. The miser, as someone once said, is usually miserable.

The arrow turned outward isn't just a spiritual posture. It's a different way of being a person.
