You Didn't Put That There

Philippians  ·  Philippians 2:13

Hook

Some of the best things about you, you didn't put there. The patience that held, the grudge you quietly stopped feeding — you didn't decide to become that.

Devotional

Writing from a prison cell, Paul tells people worn out from trying to become better that the change they're chasing was never theirs to manufacture.

"for it is God who works in you, both to will and to work for his good pleasure."

— Philippians 2:13

Read it slowly. Not just to work — to will. The wanting. God reaches underneath what you do, down to what you actually want, and goes to work there. Which explains the strange evidence: a softness where last year there was a wall, a pull toward kindness you never practiced into being.

That's not willpower having a good day. Those are fingerprints on your own desires that don't match your hands.

Application

Most of us are exhausted from trying to engineer a better version of ourselves — the new plan, the new resolve — and most of it doesn't survive an ordinary week.

So try a different question. Not what do I have to force myself to become — but what's already quietly shifting in me that I never asked for? The small mercy. The patience. The thing you keep catching yourself doing that the old you wouldn't have.

You don't have to have it sorted out to notice it. Just be honest enough to admit some of it didn't come from you.
